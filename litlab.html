What would you like to know?
It's a practical class offering experiential credit for actually work, in this case, consulting with real world clients (e.g., legal aid organizations, courts, firms, and non-profits) on legal tech or data science projects. LIT stands for Legal Innovation and Technology. Basically, it's like a mini clinic.

We meet weekly in the LIT Lab for up to 2 hours. In that time, we review the status of tasks started the prior week and choose tasks to work on for the upcoming week. We ask: what went well; what needs improvement; and what next? In this way we take a project, break it into bite-sized tasks, and work our way through it with continuous evaluation.

The specifics of work vary based on the needs of our clients (e.g., legal aid organizations, courts, firms, and non-profits). We might be mapping a client's workflow, building a website, or wrangling data.

We're not dogmatic about it, but we lean towards a Kanban implementation of Agile principles. Sometimes it looks like Scrum. A large part of what you learn is how to work as part of interdisciplinary teams, and these are the methods known to a lot of software folks.
